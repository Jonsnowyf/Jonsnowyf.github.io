ABOUT ME

Welcome to my personal website. I am currently a PhD student in Economics at the National University of Singapore (NUS). My research interests span labor economics, immigration, international trade, and general equilibrium applications.


Previously, I served as a full-time Research Assistant in Economics at the University of Hong Kong, working under the supervision of Professor Uta Schönberg. With a strong foundation in economic research and a passion for exploring complex economic phenomena, I aim to contribute meaningful insights to the field.

RESEARCH

Labor Market Segmentation with Rationed Entry: Theory and Mechanisms
This paper develops a dynamic equilibrium framework to analyze labor market segmentation characterized by rationed premium segments. We introduce a probability-based entry mechanism and an O-ring type ability aggregator to explain wage premiums despite excess supply.
PDF
A General Equilibrium Model for Inventories
This paper develops a multi-country, multi-sector production network model incorporating firms' inventory holdings. It uncovers a unique propagation mechanism for global shocks distinct from international trade channels.
PDF
Revisiting Fixed Production Costs in Multinational Production
Extending the Costinot et al. (2020) framework to incorporate multinational production and FDI. The analysis provides new insights into the interactions between consumption taxation and production subsidies.
Tax Competition, Endogenous Taxation and Business Environment
Extends the Zodrow-Mieszkowski-Wilson (ZMW) model by incorporating a transitional corporation and endogenous business environment, analyzing strategic interactions between governments and firms.
